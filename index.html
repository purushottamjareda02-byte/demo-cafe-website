Our story

Coffee, treated
with patience.

Maison Noir began as a single roaster's bench and a handful of regulars. Today it remains small by choice — every cup pulled to order, every pastry baked before dawn, every table set for someone who needs to slow down.

We work directly with three family farms across Ethiopia, Colombia and Sumatra, roasting in small batches twice weekly so what you taste is always close to harvest.

Elena Voss
Founder & Head Roaster

The craft behind every pour

01

Sourced

Green beans bought direct from three family farms, paying well above commodity price — with full traceability to the harvest lot.

02

Roasted

Small batches, twice weekly, profiled by hand over an open-drum roaster to coax out each lot's own character.

03

Rested

Every batch rests a minimum of five days before it's ground, letting CO₂ off-gas fully so flavour settles.

04

Poured

Brewed to order on calibrated equipment, dialled in fresh each morning. We pull, taste, adjust — before a single cup reaches a guest.
